# Collaborating with Colleagues

Share content and work together with other teachers.

## Permission Model

Eduskript uses **no-access-by-default**. Adding someone as a collaborator doesn't automatically share your content — you must explicitly grant access.

## Permission Levels

| Level | Can do |
|-------|--------|
| **Author** | Edit, delete, manage permissions |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access |

## Sharing Content

1. Open a collection, skript, or page
2. Click **Share** or the permissions icon
3. Search for a colleague by email
4. Select permission level (Author or Viewer)
5. Save

The colleague now sees that content in their dashboard.

## What Gets Shared

Permissions work at each level:

| Share level | They can access |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Collection | The collection and its published skripts |
| Skript | That skript and its pages |
| Page | Just that page |

Sharing a collection doesn't automatically share unpublished skripts inside it.

## Finding Collaborators

Colleagues must have an Eduskript account. Search by:
- Email address
- Username (page slug)

## Co-Teaching

For true co-teaching:
1. Share a collection with **Author** permission
2. Both teachers can create/edit content
3. Content appears on both teachers' dashboards

The content URL uses the original owner's page slug.

## Removing Access

1. Open the content's share settings
2. Find the collaborator
3. Click **Remove**

They immediately lose access.

## Tips

- Share at the collection level for courses you fully co-teach
- Use viewer access for peer review
- Keep sensitive draft content unshared until ready
