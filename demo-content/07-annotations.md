# Annotations & Broadcasting

Draw on top of any page — like marking up a printed handout, except your students see it live on their own devices.

---

## Drawing

Pen tool in the toolbar. Multiple colors and pen sizes. Erase precisely. Annotations save automatically, reposition when you edit the page (so they stay anchored to the right paragraphs).

By default: **personal**. Only you see them.

---

## Live broadcasting — the killer feature

```
            ┌──────────────┐
            │  Your screen │
            │   ✏️ draws    │
            └──────┬───────┘
                   │
        ┌──────────┼──────────┐
        ▼          ▼          ▼
   ┌────────┐ ┌────────┐ ┌────────┐
   │Student │ │Student │ │Student │
   │  sees  │ │  sees  │ │  sees  │
   │  live  │ │  live  │ │  live  │
   └────────┘ └────────┘ └────────┘
```

Switch to **broadcast mode** — every stroke streams to every student in real time, on their own device. No projector needed. Students keep your annotations after class for review.

**Three scopes:**
- **Class** — stream to one class (like a shared whiteboard)
- **Individual** — stream to one student (personalized feedback in a lab)
- **Public** — visible to every page visitor (great for errata or "see also" notes)

Latency under 100ms in normal network conditions. Works on flaky WiFi (reconnects gracefully, no lost strokes).

---

## Sticky notes & snaps

**Sticky note** — drop anywhere, color-code, minimize. Great for "re-record this before next semester" reminders.

**Snap** — draw a rectangle around part of the page, annotate it separately as its own overlay. Useful for pulling a diagram out of context, or building an annotated walkthrough.

---

## Workflow for a live class

1. Open your lesson
2. Switch to **class broadcast** mode
3. Draw, highlight, circle as you talk
4. Students follow on phone / tablet / laptop
5. Your annotations persist for post-class review

No projector = no problem. Students at the back, students with vision issues, and remote attendees all see your work clearly.
