# Annotations, Sticky Notes and Snaps

Draw directly on your content to highlight, explain, and give feedback — like writing on a whiteboard, but layered on top of your digital lesson.

---

## Drawing Annotations

Select the pen tool in the toolbar and draw on any page. Your annotations are saved automatically and reposition themselves when the page layout changes.

- **Personal annotations**: Only you see them — great for lecture prep
- **Multiple colors and pen sizes**: Organize your markup visually
- **Eraser tool**: Clean up with precision

## Sticky Notes

Place sticky notes anywhere on a page to add comments, reminders, or instructions. They can be minimized, moved, and colored.

## Snaps

Take a snapshot of a section of the page and annotate it separately — useful for creating focused explanations of a specific diagram or code block.

## Broadcasting

This is where it gets powerful for the classroom:

> [!abstract] Broadcast Modes
> - **Class broadcast**: Your annotations appear on every student's screen in real-time — like drawing on a shared whiteboard
> - **Individual feedback**: Send annotations to a specific student — perfect for personalized feedback on their work
> - **Public annotations**: Visible to everyone visiting the page, even without logging in

> [!tip] Classroom Workflow
> 1. Open a page during class
> 2. Switch to broadcast mode
> 3. Draw, highlight, circle important parts
> 4. Every student sees your annotations live on their device
> 5. After class, students still have the annotations for review
